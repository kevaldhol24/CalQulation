Last month, my neighbor was bragging about his new home loan—a floating rate loan that started at 8.5%, nearly a full percentage point below my fixed 9.4%. "I'm saving ₹12,000 every month compared to what you're paying," he told me over our fence.

I didn't argue, but I couldn't help thinking about what he might be saying three years from now.

The truth about floating rate loans is rarely discussed fully during those exciting moments of loan approval. While the initial rate often looks attractive, the long-term reality can be dramatically different. I've personally experienced this rollercoaster, watching my first home loan's EMI jump by 40% over four years as rates climbed.

### The Psychological Impact: Rate Anxiety

Beyond the financial calculations lies something equally important: your peace of mind.

<Callout type="warning">
  According to a 2024 Indian Housing Finance Association survey, 68% of floating rate borrowers report checking interest rate news at least weekly, with 42% saying rate changes negatively impact their mental well-being.
</Callout>

This constant monitoring creates what psychologists now call "rate anxiety"—a persistent concern about factors beyond your control that directly impact your financial stability.

Is saving a few thousand per month in the early years worth the psychological burden of payment uncertainty for a decade? For many, the answer is increasingly "no."
